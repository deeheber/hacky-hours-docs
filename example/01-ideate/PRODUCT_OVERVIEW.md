## Who — Target Audience

**Primary user: the everyday neighbor** — someone who lives in a specific neighborhood and wants to know what's happening nearby without wading through the noise of general social media. They're not particularly tech-savvy, they're busy, and they check their phone more than their email. They don't want to download another app. They want a simple, clean place to see: "What's happening on my block this week?"

**Secondary user: the neighborhood organizer** — someone who runs community events (block parties, cleanups, lost pet alerts) and currently struggles to get the word out. They're frustrated by how much effort it takes to reach people through Facebook, email, or flyers. They want one place to post and know residents will actually see it.

**Non-user (for now):** businesses, advertisers, people outside the neighborhood. This is strictly for residents.

## What — Product Description

NeighborBoard is a simple, private web-based community bulletin board for a single neighborhood. Residents can:
- **Browse events and announcements** — a clean, chronological feed with no algorithm
- **Post events** — with a title, description, date/time, and location (optional)
- **Post announcements** — for non-time-bound notices (lost pet, free furniture, road closure)
- **RSVP to events** — so organizers can see expected attendance

There is no algorithmic feed, no advertising, no public profiles, and no content from outside the neighborhood.

## Where — Platform and Delivery

A **mobile-friendly web app** — accessible from any browser on any device. No app download required. Hosted in the cloud so residents don't need to install anything.

The primary experience is designed for mobile browsers (where most people check local news) with a clean desktop view as well.

## When — Timeline and Priority

**Goal: a working MVP in 6 weeks**, piloted with one neighborhood (~200 households). The pilot will run for 3 months to validate whether residents actually use it before investing in V1 features. There is no hard external deadline — this is self-funded and community-driven.

## Why — Value and Motivation

Local community connection is weakening in part because the tools for it are broken. Facebook groups are noisy, algorithm-driven, and increasingly distrusted. Email lists are one-way and hard to join. Physical flyers don't reach renters or people who work long hours.

NeighborBoard exists to fill that gap: a quiet, simple, trusted space that's just for your block. No engagement metrics, no data harvesting, no ads. The goal is connection, not attention.

The builder lives in the target neighborhood, has personally missed events due to the current information gap, and has heard the same frustration from neighbors.

---

## Non-Goals

- No integration with social media platforms
- No advertising or monetization in the MVP or V1
- No support for multiple neighborhoods in the MVP (single neighborhood only)
- No native mobile app — web only for MVP and V1
- No direct messaging between residents
- No business listings or commercial posts
