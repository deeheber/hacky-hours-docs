## Ideas and Notes

- I keep missing events in my neighborhood — farmer's market moved times, block party I didn't know about, lost dog notices I saw too late
- Facebook groups are chaos. Too much noise. Algorithm decides what I see.
- Email chains are one-way — only works if you're already on the list
- There's no dedicated place for just our neighborhood
- What if it was as simple as a bulletin board? Like a physical corkboard but online
- Maybe people post "events" with a date/time and "announcements" without one
- Should probably be able to RSVP so organizers know how many people are coming
- I'd want to get a notification when something new is posted — but NOT constant emails
- Privacy is a concern — I don't want strangers knowing when I'm away from home for a neighborhood event (implies my house is empty)
- Who gets to post? Anyone in the neighborhood? Or just verified residents? How do you even verify that?
- Should it be one app for all neighborhoods or separate per neighborhood?
- If it's separate per neighborhood, how do you find yours?
- I'm thinking web app first — everyone has a browser, don't need to convince people to download an app
- Mobile-friendly is a must though — people check their phones
- Monetization? Not now. This is for the community. Maybe donations eventually.
- Could start with just my neighborhood as a pilot — like 200 households
- What about moderation? Who removes spam or inappropriate posts? Probably a neighborhood admin role
- Could have a "block captain" or similar who manages the neighborhood's board
- Names: NeighborBoard? BlockBoard? CorkBoard? LocalBoard? I like NeighborBoard.
- Could integrate with existing tools? Nextdoor exists but it's too big and corporate. This would be hyper-local and simple.
- What does the MVP actually need? Just: post events, see events, RSVP. Everything else is extra.

## Inspirations and References

- Physical community bulletin boards at the library, coffee shop, laundromat
- Nextdoor (but simpler, less corporate, no algorithmic feed)
- Meetup.com (but for neighborhoods, not interest groups)
- Old-school email listservs (the directness, not the interface)

## Questions I Don't Have Answers To Yet

- How does a new neighborhood get set up? Who creates it?
- How do you verify someone actually lives in a neighborhood? (Or do you trust people to self-select?)
- What happens when someone moves away? Do they lose access?
- Do we need a mobile app eventually, or is a good mobile web experience enough?
- How do we handle neighborhoods that overlap (apartment buildings on a street boundary)?
- What's the moderation model — do we need it for MVP?
- Is RSVP count public (anyone can see "14 people are going") or private (only organizer sees)?

## What Success Looks Like

My neighborhood uses it for a full year without me having to maintain it constantly. Residents post their own events. New neighbors find it and join without needing a personal invitation. The block party has better turnout because people actually knew about it.
